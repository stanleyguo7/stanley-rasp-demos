Semi-final Tossups
(1)In 1992, Copa Airlines Flight 201 crashed in this region, which was first crossed overland by a Land Rover dubbed "The Affectionate Cockroach." This region begins from the terminus town of Yaviza all the way to the town of Turbo, and is partially dominated by the Atrato River delta. This area has been notorious for harboring FARC [[fark]] insurgents and drug smugglers. For the point, name this "gap" in the Pan-American Highway which corresponds to the break in the North and South American continents.
ANSWER: ‎Darién Gap (or Tapón del Darién)
(2)A blood-drinking creature called the Vrykolatios [[vrih-koh-lah-TEE-ohs]] was said to live on this island. This island is part of a namesake caldera which also contains the uninhabited islands of Nea Kameni and Palea Kameni. It's not Cyprus, but the Minoan eruption of about 3,600 years ago destroyed the ancient settlement of Akrotiri [[ak-roe-TIH-ree]] on this island. For the point, name this southernmost island of the Cyclades [["sigh"-kluh-DEEZ]] group, a popular tourist destination in Greece due to its white-and-blue-domed buildings.
ANSWER: Santorini (accept Thira)
(3)Dum aloo is a popular dish in this multi-national region prepared by the Pandits [[PAHN-deets]]. One country claims that the recently-established Union Territory of Ladakh includes a portion of this region known as Aksai Chin. This region, which is often defined as lying between the Pir Panjal and Karakoram [[kah-rah-KOH-rahm]] ranges, is split by the informally designated Line of Control. One part of this region is administered as a part of a joint region with Jammu. For the point, name this disputed territory administered by China, Pakistan, and India.
ANSWER: Kashmir (accept Jammu and Kashmir)
(4)The Jakhaveti [[jah-kah-VEH-tee]] Plateau sits within this mountain range and is crossed via the Abul-Samsari range as part of this range's "Lesser" mountains. This range runs through disputed territories such as South Ossetia [[oh-SET-yah]], Abkhazia [[ahb-KAH-zyah]], and Nagorno-Karabakh [[nah-GOR-noh kah-rah-BAHK]]. Europe's highest mountain is located in the western part of this range, which runs between the Black and Caspian Seas. For the point, name this mountain range that defines the boundary between Europe and Asia.
ANSWER: Caucasus Mountains
(5)A now-abandoned town along this road paid homage to silent film star William S. Hart with its name of Two Guns. This route crosses directly through both the Black Mountains and the polychromatic Painted Desert. Many Okies fleeing the “Dust Bowl” of the 1930s used this highway to flee west. For the point, name this historical “route” that ran from Chicago to Santa Monica, California.
ANSWER: US Route 66
(6)“Spirit of Bujabald [[BOO-jah-bald]]” is a mythical figure manifested in a man that lives next to this river’s Bujagali [[boo-jah-GAH-lee]] Falls. This river’s namesake species of perch decimated the population of the nearby Lake Kwania’s [[KWAH-nyahs]] native stock of fish. This tributary, named for the color it gets from a heavy presence of clay, flows through Lake Kyoga [[kai-OH-gah]] and Lake Albert and has its source at Lake Victoria. For the point, name this branch of the longest river in the world that is contrasted with another tributary, the Blue Nile.
ANSWER: White Nile (prompt on partial answer)
(7)The Chinijo [[chee-NEE-ho]] archipelago is administered by an island in this territory called Lanzarote [[lan-zah-ROH-teh]]. This territory's Guanche [[GWAHN-cheh]] people include residents of La Gomera who are famed for their whistle speech. Mount Teide [[teh-EE-deh]], the tallest mountain in Spain, is located on this region's largest island, Tenerife [[teh-neh-REE-feh]]. For the point, name this Spanish archipelago off the coast of Morocco, which lends its name to a type of yellow bird.
ANSWER: Canary Islands (accept Islas Canarias; accept The Canaries)
(8)After a 2021 failure of this system, deregulation policies allowed providers to charge bills over $10,000. ANSWER: Texas Power Grid failure (accept Texas Interconnection; accept 2021 Texas Power Crisis; accept descriptive answers indicating the failure of the power or electrical system in the state of Texas))
(9)This mountain's glaciers, which are threatened by ash deposits from Tungurahua [[toon-goo-RAH-hwah]], are the source of the Ambato and Chambo Rivers. (10)In Dawa County in this historical region, crimson grass grows all over Red Beach in Panjin Shi. This region's northern border was set in the Treaty of Nerchinsk.
